Primary Sprint 15 goals:

A. Implement real signature-grade trust
- Add a real signed-evidence verification path.
- Support at least one concrete signed artifact or signed bundle workflow.
- Bind trust to configured signers or trusted signing policy.
- Do not mark evidence `signature_verified` unless a real signature check passes.
- Expose machine-readable signature failure reasons.

B. Add signer-trust-aware verification policy
- Extend evidence verification so trust can depend on:
  - metadata policy
  - provider byte verification
  - signature verification
- Preserve provider verification as a lower trust tier than signature verification when policy says so.
- Allow stricter environments to require `signature_verified`.
- Surface the exact required tier and the actual achieved tier clearly.

C. Add tamper-evident dossier archive bundles
- Materialize dossier review into a durable archive package.
- Bind archive identity to:
  - manifest identity
  - dossier hash
  - archive hash or seal
- Include enough core assets for later review without relying only on live DB state.
- Make archive generation deterministic enough for later verification.

D. Add policy-versioned verification provenance
- Capture the policy version used during evaluation.
- Capture which verification path was used:
  - metadata
  - provider
  - signature
- Preserve enough provenance to later explain why a promotion passed or failed.
- Expose this in dossiers and archive outputs.

E. Add stricter environment assurance rules
- Allow environments to require:
  - provider verification
  - signature verification
  - archive generation
  - combinations of the above
- Keep staging/lower environments more flexible.
- Make blockers deterministic and machine-readable.

F. Harden emergency and rollback proof
- Ensure rollback and break-glass paths still preserve:
  - signature/archive expectations when required
  - incident lineage
  - after-action linkage
  - compensating manifest linkage
- Do not allow emergency paths to silently drop assurance requirements without explicit policy outcome.

G. Preserve enterprise-grade focus
- Only add runtime surface area if it materially improves signed trust, archive durability, or decision provenance.
- Do not broaden product scope.
- Avoid generic plugin sprawl.
