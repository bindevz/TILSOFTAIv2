The platform is already enterprise-grade and now has a real high-assurance posture.
However, it still has operational trust-infrastructure debt because:
- trusted signers are still mainly static config entries,
- signer rotation/revocation lifecycle is not yet a first-class governed operation,
- archive storage is still filesystem-centric,
- archive packages are not yet re-verified through a first-class verification flow,
- trust-store evolution and archive operations are not yet surfaced strongly enough for long-lived audit interpretability,
- and the platform still relies too much on current configuration state for some trust explanations.

Sprint 16 mission:
Turn the platform from “high-assurance in repository/runtime behavior” into “high-assurance in trust operations and archive durability”.

Primary objective:
1. Add signer lifecycle semantics.
2. Add governed trust-store mutation.
3. Add stronger archive backend/storage boundaries.
4. Add archive verification/replay capability.
5. Preserve historical interpretability under signer lifecycle evolution.
6. Strengthen trust-store and archive provenance in review surfaces.
7. Keep Sprint 16 focused on operationalized trust infrastructure, not feature work.

Architecture principles:
1. `ISupervisorRuntime` remains the runtime orchestration owner.
2. Platform catalog remains the production source-of-truth.
3. Promotion gates, manifests, attestations, evidence trust, and dossier archives remain mandatory release primitives.
4. `provider_verified` and `signature_verified` remain valid and must not regress.
5. Signer trust must evolve from static configuration to lifecycle-managed trust operations.
6. Archive packaging must remain durable and reviewable even as storage backends evolve.
7. Historical signed releases must remain interpretable after signer rotation or revocation.
8. Governed mutation discipline must apply to trust-store changes, not only catalog record changes.
9. Sprint 16 success is measured by operationalized trust infrastructure, not runtime novelty.
10. Do not turn Sprint 16 into a feature sprint.

Primary Sprint 16 goals:

A. Add signer lifecycle semantics
- Introduce signer lifecycle concepts such as:
  - active
  - rotated
  - revoked
  - retired
- Support multiple keys per signer or explicit key rotation semantics.
- Add validity windows for signer keys where appropriate.
- Preserve enough historical signer metadata so old releases remain auditable.

B. Add governed trust-store mutation
- Signer and trust-store changes must not be ad hoc config edits.
- Add governed mutation or an equivalent controlled path for signer trust changes.
- Audit signer additions, rotations, revocations, and removals.
- Require explicit review/approval semantics for trust-store changes.

C. Add stronger archive backend/storage boundaries
- Decouple archive packaging from archive storage location assumptions.
- Keep the current filesystem backend working.
- Introduce a boundary that makes additional durable backends possible without redesign.
- Keep scope tight and concrete.

D. Add archive verification/replay
- Add a flow to re-open and verify archived dossier packages later.
- Validate archive hash/seal deterministically.
- Return machine-readable mismatch or corruption signals.
- Make the verification path usable by operators and auditors.

E. Preserve historical interpretability
- Historical releases must remain understandable even if signer keys rotate or become revoked later.
- Keep signer/key metadata or provenance linkage sufficient for later review.
- Ensure emergency and rollback releases remain interpretable under trust-store evolution.

F. Strengthen review surfaces
- Expose signer lifecycle, key id, trust-store provenance, archive backend/source, and verification outcomes in dossier or related review APIs.
- Emit warnings when historical signer state changed after verification in ways relevant to audit.
- Keep outputs operator-usable and machine-readable.

G. Preserve enterprise-grade focus
- Only add surface area that materially improves trust operations, archive durability, or historical interpretability.
- Do not broaden product scope.
- Avoid generic plugin or marketplace sprawl.
